The KCG / KCGI Story

The Kyoto College of Graduate Studies for Informatics (KCGI), established in 2004 as Japan's first IT professional graduate school, continues the legacy of its parent institution, KCG (Kyoto Computer Gakuin), which was founded in 1963 as Japan's first computer education institution. Our mission is to produce highly skilled informatics professionals capable of leading global innovation. KCGI specifically focuses on applied information technology within the context of management and entrepreneurship.

We are proud to stand at the intersection of technology's future and Kyoto's deep cultural history.

History of Kyoto: The Ancient Capital

Kyoto served as Japan's imperial capital for over a thousand years (794 to 1868), originally named Heian-kyō. It is the heart of traditional Japanese culture, renowned for its 17 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including temples, shrines, and gardens. Unlike many other major Japanese cities, Kyoto was largely spared from the destruction of WWII, preserving its historical integrity.

Studying here means engaging daily with this living history.

Kyoto Practical Guide: Culture & Essentials

Essential guides covering cultural rules, utilities, and daily life logistics for a smooth transition.

Cultural Rules & Etiquette

Key practices to follow for respectful living in Japan.

Punctuality is Key

Being on time is the standard. For classes, meetings, and even social plans, aim to arrive 5-10 minutes early.

Greetings & Bowing

A simple nod or a slight bow (Eshaku) is common and shows respect in academic and professional settings.

Learn Etiquette: Japan Guide (Basic Etiquette)
Shoe Removal

Always remove shoes at the entrance (genkan) of homes, temples, and some traditional restaurants. Switch to the provided indoor slippers.

"kokoka" Student Membership

Register at the "kokoka" Kyoto International Community House. They offer free legal/visa consultations, Japanese language classes, and the kokoka Family Program to match you with local families.

Check events: kokoka Official Site

Food, Utilities & Budgeting

Managing housing essentials, waste, and culinary exploration.

"Okoshiyasu Pass" (Student Perk)

Get the "Ryugakusei Okoshiyasu Pass"! It gives international students free entry to Nijo Castle, Kyoto Tower, the Zoo, Kyoto International Manga Museum, Botanical Gardens, and 15+ more attractions.

Learn more: Kyoto Int'l Foundation
Waste Collection

Garbage separation is strict. You MUST follow your local ward's calendar for Burnable, Non-Burnable, and Recyclables days.

Official waste rules: Kyoto City Guide
Utilities Setup

Set up water, electricity, and gas contracts immediately. Bills can be paid easily at any convenience store (konbini).

Electricity: KEPCO (Kansai Electric)
Local Supermarkets

For budgeting, shop at local supermarkets like Fresco, Gyomu Super (for bulk), or Izumiya. Look for discount stickers in the evening.

Seasonal Events

Sakura Illuminations (Late Mar - Apr)

Many temples and parks, like Maruyama Park, light up the cherry blossom trees for magical nighttime viewing (yozakura).

Aoi Matsuri (May 15)

One of Kyoto's three great festivals, featuring a large procession in elegant Heian period aristocratic clothing.

Gion Matsuri (July)

Japan's most famous festival. The highlight is the grand procession of massive, ornate yamaboko floats.

Gozan Okuribi (Aug 16)

Giant bonfires in the shape of kanji characters are lit on the mountains surrounding Kyoto to guide ancestral spirits.

Jidai Matsuri (Oct 22)

"Festival of the Ages." A grand parade showcasing costumes from every major period of Japanese history.

Arashiyama Hanatouro (December)

A winter illumination event where thousands of lanterns light up the Bamboo Grove and Togetsukyo Bridge.

Kyoto by Season

Spring (Mar - May)

The city is covered in pink cherry blossoms (sakura). Key events include Aoi Matsuri and temple illuminations.

Summer (Jun - Aug)

Hot and humid, but vibrant with festivals like Gion Matsuri and Gozan Okuribi. Enjoy *kawa-doko* (dining over the river).

Autumn (Sep - Nov)

Spectacular autumn foliage (*momiji*). Temples are bathed in red and gold. See the Jidai Matsuri parade.

Winter (Dec - Feb)

Cold and quiet, offering a peaceful atmosphere. See the Arashiyama Hanatouro and hope for a rare, beautiful snow day.

Logistics & Mapping: Settling In

Official and practical steps for settling in, managing residency, and mastering local navigation.

Residency & Banking

Your first official steps in Japan.

Ward Office Registration (Critical)

You must register your address at your local Ward Office (Kuyakusho) within 14 days of moving in to get your Residence Card updated.

Zairyu Card Info: Immigration Services Agency (ISA)
National Health Insurance (NHI)

Enrollment in the National Health Insurance (Kokumin Kenkō Hoken) is mandatory. You will do this at the Ward Office at the same time.

Opening a Bank Account

You need your updated Residence Card and Student ID. Japan Post Bank (Yūcho) is often the easiest for new students. MEXT scholars must use it.

Bank Info: Japan Post Bank
Part-Time Work Limit

Your student visa allows part-time work (with permission) up to 28-hours per week during term, or 40 hours per week during long vacations (summer, winter, spring).

Work Permit Rules: ISA (Permission)

Transport & Mobile Phones

Getting connected and getting around Kyoto.

Student Commuter Pass (Teiki-ken)

Get a Tsugaku Shomeisho (commuter certificate) from a campus machine. Take it to a ticket office (e.g., at Kyoto Station) to buy a heavily discounted commuter pass for the shortest route between your home and campus.

Kyoto Bus Routes: Kyoto City Bus Official Site
IC Card (ICOCA)

Get an ICOCA card at Kyoto Station or any JR West station. It works seamlessly on all buses, subways, and JR trains in the region. iPhone users can also add ICOCA to Apple Pay.

ICOCA Card Info: JR West ICOCA
Getting a Mobile Phone

SIM-free phones (from Apple, Bic Camera, etc.) plus a monthly plan from an MVNO (like IIJmio or Rakuten Mobile) is often cheapest. Major carriers (Docomo, AU) require longer contracts.

Bicycle Use & Registration

Bicycles are very common. You MUST register your bike (to prevent theft) and park only in designated areas. Riding on the left with traffic is the law.

Cycling Rules: Kyoto City Bicycle Rules
